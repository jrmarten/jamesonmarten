coaching

    coaching executives

    Over the past 25 years we have successfully coached hundreds of executives. Although they often ask us to address "soft" skills, coaching success is always on business results. Coachees often need to face blind spots and deal with their fears. They must trust that they are working with a seasoned professional who understands the reality of their world. Each executive has unique needs. A high-potential manager may need to learn to "get it", while a C-suite executive may need to balance their ego with more finesse. The impact of effective coaching can be measured with 360° feedback and manager's ratings of project assignments.

    coaching teams

    Traditionally called "team building", team coaching is a far cry from touchy-feely boondoggles of the past. Our focus is always on strategy and/or execution. Although this often requires attention to people issues — such as trust, collaboration and straight-talk — the goal is always improving the business.

    Teams learn best as teams, whether senior teams or project teams. To learn new skills, teams must wrestle with real issues and real people. When we design a team-coaching program, we show the group how to use practical techniques to create measurable results.
