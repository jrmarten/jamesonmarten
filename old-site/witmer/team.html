Neil Witmer, Ph.D.

Neil founded Witmer & Associates in 1987, and has consulted in over thirty industries since 1983. He partners with CEOs and private-equity boards to provide talent solutions to execution problems. He assesses high-potential managers for hire, promotion and career development. He coaches top executives to be effective leaders and high potentials to accelerate their careers. Other capabilities include talent metrics, 360° surveys, employee engagement strategy and helping organizations to evaluate the operating potential of acquisitions.

Neil has been an adjunct consultant to an international quality consulting firm, helping senior executives apply leadership to process improvement. He has conducted workshops internationally on the "human" aspects of organizational change and reengineering.

Neil is the co-creator of Unleashing The Leader Within™, a nationally acclaimed leadership coaching program that produces eyebrow-raising impacts on both leaders and organizations. He is co-designer of Pinpoint Performance™, an online metrics system that allows top leaders to monitor employee alignment, engagement and competence.

Neil has authored numerous articles and management tools, including Diagnosing Performance, CEO Skill Profile, The Misunderstood Values of the CEO and Have Your Senior Managers Outgrown Leadership Training? His book, I GET IT!, outlines 5 simplified fundamentals that distinguish managers who get it from those who don't.

Neil and wife Mary Pat share their "empty" nest near Chicago with a variety of transient college students and dogs. His hobbies include scuba diving, bush-league woodworking and storytelling.

Neil received a BA in Psychology from Kalamazoo College, a MA in Human Development from the University of Chicago, and a PhD in Industrial/Organizational Psychology from NCSU.




Jeffrey Grip, Ph.D.

Jeff joined Witmer & Associates in 1995 and currently runs the East Coast office. He specializes in helping executives build teams that can execute their vision. Jeff assesses managers for hire, promotion and career development. He also conducts deep developmental coaching to accelerate the career development of talented executives. He consults with corporations in the areas of organizational health, employee engagement, leadership effectiveness, and company-wide collaboration. A major focus of his work is the relationship between the personal development of executives and the effectiveness of their leadership.

Prior to 1995, Jeff served as President and CEO of the Chicago School of Professional Psychology, where he was responsible for business operations, financial and strategic planning, personnel recruitment and development, and marketing. Revenue quadrupled during his tenure. He established a reputation for fostering personal growth and development, and for successful conflict resolution.

Jeff has a long history of volunteer activity; he currently chairs the board of trustees of the Frank Lloyd Wright Foundation. He lives near Baltimore with his wife, Lynne, and a small menagerie of pets and foster animals. His children and grandchildren consume much of his time and love. On those rare occasions when he has the time, he enjoys sailing on Chesapeake Bay. Other pursuits include travel, cooking and home renovation.

Jeff received a BA in Psychology from Wesleyan University, and a PhD in Psychology from the University of Chicago.




Steve Sherwood

Steve Sherwood is co-creator and head coach of Witmer & Associates' acclaimed Unleashing The Leader Within™. He has spent the last 25 years as a master facilitator of programs such as Life Success Seminars' Interpersonal Intensive, Personal Power and Results Now! Steve specializes in helping people grasp the underlying motives behind their behavior, both excellence and folly. Steve's protegés are known for achieving breakthroughs in their careers and personal lives.

Steve has managed a variety of training organizations and sales teams. He has designed and facilitated experiential education programs for the travel industry, health care and consumer products. He coaches executives and CEOs to evaluate career transitions.

Steve lives in Macon, Georgia with his wife Lisa. Together they import and restore 19th century antique furniture.




Paul De Young, Ph.D.

Paul has over 25 years of international consulting experience. He provides services in business growth, organizational diagnosis and surveys, measurement and design, leadership assessment/selection and coaching, talent management and team development. He previously held positions with Deloitte Consulting, Towers Watson, Children's Hospital of Orange County, International Survey Research, Richard Chang Associates and the Navy Personnel Research and Development Center.

He has consulted to over 100 companies, large and small, across a broad spectrum of industries including technology, communications and media, bio/pharmaceuticals, manufacturing, public sector and higher education, retail, utilities, financial services, and healthcare. Some of these include: United Nations, University of California, Walt Disney Corp., Bristol-Myers Squibb, Nestlé, Salt River Project, Toyota Motor Sales, T-Mobile, HP, American Express, State Farm, Honeywell, and Kaiser Permanente.

Paul is certified as a leadership coach in several tools including: TalentSmart's emotional intelligence (EQ) tests and assessments, Hogan and SHL personality instruments and Lominger's assessment, development and competency tools. He is a TalentSmart Master Certified presenter in emotional intelligence. Paul's facilitation style is warm and genuine, along with a good dose of humor. He speaks regularly at local and international associations including ASTD and the HR Leadership Summit. Topics include: leadership in the new economy, sustainable employee engagement, eliminating performance ratings, and achieving personal-work harmony. He has written broadly on the topics of employee engagement, leadership, talent management, workforce planning, high-tech talent, gainsharing and becoming an employer of choice. He co-authored the book Measuring Organizational Improvement Impact.

Paul received a BA in Psychology from University of California, Irvine, and a MA, PhD in Industrial/Organizational Psychology from California School of Professional Psychology.
